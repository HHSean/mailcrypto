Send crypto to any email address.

No need for a bank account or their wallet address. Mailcrypto is a decentralized, non-custodial protocol that lets you send ERC-20s and ERC-721s to any email address. It's free, we take no fees.

Set a password

Select the amount you want to send, set a key phrase, and enter the recipient's email. Your funds will be stored securely and non-custodially in the smart contract. We'll send a notification to the recipient telling him his funds are ready. If he never claims them, you can always get them back.

Recipient gets email

Your beneficiary gets notified of incoming funds. They can then either select to claim the funds to an existing wallet address, or set up a new one on the spot where we'll send the funds to.

How this works.

You want to send crypto (Ethereum, or ERC-20 tokens, or ERC-721s) to someone. But you don't know their address. Or maybe they don't have one. Or maybe they hate crypto. Doesn't matter. You just need to know their email address, and a pass phrase that is shared between sender and recipient.

Let's walk through what happens, in order. The sender deposits an amount of tokens in the smart contract, together with a keccak256 hashed passphrase and a recipient email address. An event is emmited, and our decentralized backend picks it up and sends weekly emails to the recipient email address to check their incoming funds.

The recipient goes to claim the funds. They enter the confidential passphrase, and the transaction gets sent through the flashots RPC endpoint to not get frontrun. The funds either go to an existing wallet address that they may have, or else we set one up for them on the spot. That's it, funds transferred.
